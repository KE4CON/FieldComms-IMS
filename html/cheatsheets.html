🚨 NTS / ARRL Message Precedence
EMERGENCY
Any message having life-or-death urgency. Takes absolute priority over all other traffic. Declared by the originating station. Any amateur may break into a net to pass emergency traffic. Identify with "EMERGENCY" before the message.
PRIORITY (P)
Important messages having some urgency that should be handled as quickly as possible. Situations that are serious but where life is not immediately at risk. Examples: officials need routing, critical supply requests.
WELFARE (W)
Message intended to inquire into the health and welfare of individuals, or messages between individuals in or near disaster areas. Passed after EMERGENCY and PRIORITY traffic clears.
ROUTINE (R)
Normal traffic that is not urgent or time-sensitive. Processed in the order received during normal net cycles.
